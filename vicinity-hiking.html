Hiking

The area along the Thaya River and Austrian border offers such gems such as Lednice-Valtice (castle garden, artificial landscape - a UNESCO site), the archaeological site of Lower Věstonice 

(where the famous Věstonice Venus was found), Mikulov (chateau, Gothic monuments , the Jewish ghetto), Pavlov (wine village with preserved folk architecture), Valtice (a baroque castle and unique landscape elements). Purely natural attractions include Pálava (CHKO and biosphere reserve and a UNESCO site), the Lednice ponds (NPR).

The Novomlýnské reservoir on the Thaya River as well as many hiking and biking trails tempt one to get to know nature and participate in water sports and active recreation.

 

Lednice

Lednice-Valtice is a unique hiking area abounding in natural, cultural, historical and technical monuments. The area is also an important Moravian wine region.

Pálava

There is nowhere else quite like Palava in the Czech Republic. Where else can you walk in the footsteps of prehistoric men along the dry preserved steppe and through riparian forests, wetlands and other rugged landscapes? The Palava Protected

Landscape Area was declared in 1976 over an area of ​​about 7,500 hectares. Its centre is the Pavlov Hills ridge with a length of 12 km and Devin, the highest peak (554 m). The head office of CHKO is at Náměstí 32  in Mikulov, chko@palava.cz.

  

Cycling paths

Hustopeče and its surroundings are bisected by a network of Moravian wine trails along which you can enjoy the beauty of southern Moravia. The trails were created recently and are a great way for tourists to combine all of the beauties that our region has to offer.
